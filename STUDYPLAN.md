# Prerequisite Roadmap for General Relativity (GR) and Quantum Field Theory (QFT)

## 1. Core Physics Foundations

These topics should be solid at an undergraduate-to-early-graduate level.
These are required for Both GR & QFT).

### Classical Mechanics (Advanced)

- [x] Newtonian mechanics (for intuition)
- Lagrangian mechanics
- Hamiltonian mechanics
- Phase space
- Poisson brackets
- Canonical transformations
- Continuous symmetries and conserved quantities
- Noether’s theorem (critical for QFT, useful for GR)

### Classical Electrodynamics
- Maxwell’s equations (integral & differential forms)
- Gauge invariance
- Potentials and gauge freedom
- Lorentz force and covariant formulation
- Stress–energy tensor (important bridge to GR)
- Radiation and waves

### Special Relativity
- Minkowski spacetime
- Lorentz transformations
- 4-vectors and tensors
- Relativistic energy–momentum
- Relativistic Lagrangians
- Causality and light cones

## 2. Quantum Mechanics (Core for QFT)
You need more than an introductory QM course.
### Non-relativistic Quantum Mechanics
- Hilbert spaces
- Linear operators
- Eigenvalue problems
- Commutation relations
- Measurement theory
- Dirac bra–ket notation
- Harmonic oscillator (ladder operators are essential)
- Angular momentum & spin
- Perturbation theory
- Scattering theory (basic level)
### Relativistic Quantum Mechanics (Bridge Topic)
- Klein–Gordon equation
- Dirac equation
- Spinors
- Probability currents
- Limitations of single-particle relativistic QM (motivation for QFT)
## 3. Mathematics Prerequisites (Critical, Especially for GR)
These are non-negotiable for real understanding.
### Linear Algebra (Advanced)
- Vector spaces (finite & infinite dimensional)
- Dual spaces
- Inner products
- Eigenvalues/eigenvectors
- Linear operators
- Change of basis
- Tensor products
- Index notation and Einstein summation
### Multivariable Calculus
- Partial derivatives
- Gradient, divergence, curl
- Multiple integrals
- Jacobians
- Coordinate transformations
- Taylor expansions in multiple variables
### Ordinary & Partial Differential Equations
- Second-order ODEs
- Boundary value problems
- Sturm–Liouville theory
- Separation of variables
- Green’s functions
- Wave, heat, and Laplace equations
### Complex Analysis (More Important for QFT)
- Complex functions
- Analyticity
- Cauchy integral theorem
- Residue theorem
- Contour integration
- Wick rotation
- Analytic continuation
## 4. Tensor Calculus & Differential Geometry (Core for GR)
This is the main mathematical barrier for GR.
### Tensor Analysis
- Tensors as multilinear maps
- Covariant vs contravariant indices
- Index raising/lowering
- Tensor contractions
- Coordinate transformations
- Metric tensor
### Differential Geometry
- Manifolds
- Charts and atlases
- Tangent and cotangent spaces
- Vector fields and differential forms
- Exterior derivative
- Lie derivative
- Covariant derivative
- Parallel transport
- Geodesics
- Curvature:
  - Riemann curvature tensor
  - Ricci tensor
  - Scalar curvature
- Levi-Civita connection
### Geometry for Physics
- Pseudo-Riemannian manifolds
- Lorentzian signature
- Geodesic deviation
- Killing vectors
- Symmetries of spacetime
## 5. Field Theory Foundations (Required for QFT)
These connect classical physics to quantum fields.
### Classical Field Theory
- Fields as dynamical variables
- Action principles for fields
- Euler–Lagrange equations for fields
- Symmetries and conserved currents
- Stress–energy tensor
- Scalar, vector, and spinor fields
### Canonical Quantization
- Quantization of classical systems
- Canonical commutation relations
- Quantization of fields
- Mode expansions
## 6. Core Quantum Field Theory Concepts
You can approach these only after the above is solid.
- Second quantization
- Creation and annihilation operators
- Free scalar, fermion, and gauge fields
- Vacuum states
- Propagators
- Time-ordered products
- Perturbation theory
- Feynman diagrams
- Scattering amplitudes
- Renormalization (conceptual level first)
- Symmetries and gauge theories
## 7. Jupyter Notebook Prerequisites (Physics-Focused)
Since you already know Python, these are the workflow skills you’ll want.
### Scientific Python Stack
- NumPy (arrays, linear algebra)
- SciPy (ODE/PDE solvers, integration)
- SymPy (symbolic math, tensors, calculus)
- Matplotlib (plots, spacetime diagrams)
- Gravipy
- Optional: einsteinpy (GR tools), xarray
### Jupyter-Specific Skills
- Markdown with LaTeX for equations
- Displaying symbolic math
- Mixing narrative + derivations + code
- Re-running and organizing computational derivations
- Using notebooks as living textbooks
### Physics-Specific Notebook Practices
- Symbolic derivation → numeric example → visualization
- Verifying analytic results numerically
- Plotting:
  - Potentials
  - Wavefunctions
  - Field configurations
  - Geodesics
- Dimensional analysis checks via code
## 8. Suggested Learning Order (Efficient Path)
- Advanced Classical Mechanics
- Linear Algebra + Multivariable Calculus (in parallel)
- Special Relativity
- Classical Electrodynamics
- Quantum Mechanics (advanced undergraduate)
- Tensor calculus & differential geometry
- Classical field theory
- General Relativity
- Relativistic QM → Quantum Field Theory
